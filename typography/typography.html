Introduction to Typography

Typography is the art and technique of arranging text so it is clear, readable, and visually appealing. It involves choosing typefaces, adjusting spacing, and organizing text in a way that helps communicate a message effectively.Designers use typography in websites, advertisements, magazines, logos, and digital media to guide the reader's attention and create a strong visual style.Good typography improves readability, strengthens branding , and helps communicate information clearly.

Key Typography Terms

  1. Typeface- the overall design of a set of characters, such as Times New Roman or Arial.
  2. Font- A specific version of a typeface that includes a particular size, weight, and style
  3. Serif- A small decorative line or stroke at the end of letters in certain typefaces.
  4. Sans Serif- A typeface that does not include the small decorative strokes found in serif fonts.
  5. Kerning- The adjustment of space between two individual letters to improve visual appeal.
  6. Leading- The vertical spacing between lines of text.
  7. Tracking- The overall spacing between groups of letters in a word or block of text.
  8. Baseline- The invisible line on which most letters sit.
  9. Ascender- The part of a lowercase letter that extends above the main body of the letter.
  10. Descender- The part of a lowercase letter that extends below the baseline.
  11. Alignment- The way text is positioned relative to a baseline or margin.
  12. Hierarchy- The organization of text in different sizes or weights to guide the reader through content.
