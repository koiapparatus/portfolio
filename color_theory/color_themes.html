Color Schemes

Showcasing various color schemes and their applications.

Color Scheme Description Color Palette Use Case
Monochromatic Uses variations of a single color including tints, shades, and tones.
Calming, sophisticated designs; brand consistency
Analogous Uses colors that are adjacent on the color wheel, typically 2-3 colors.
Harmonious, natural looks; warm or cool themes
Complementary Uses colors opposite each other on the color wheel for high contrast.
High contrast, vibrant designs; call-to-action buttons
Triadic Uses three colors equally spaced around the color wheel.
Bold, playful designs; eye-catching interfaces
Tetradic (Square) Uses four colors forming a square on the color wheel.
Complex, vibrant designs; magazine layouts
Split-Complementary Uses one color and two colors adjacent to its complement.
Balanced contrast; user-friendly interfaces
Shades & Tints Uses a base color with darker shades and lighter tints for depth.
Professional websites; hierarchical designs
Neutral Uses grays, blacks, whites, and beiges as the primary palette.
Minimalist, elegant designs; corporate branding
