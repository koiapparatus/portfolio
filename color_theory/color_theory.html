Introduction to Color Theory

Color theory is a set of principles used to understand how colors work together,how they are created, and how they effect perception and emotion. Artists, designers, photographers, adn digital creators use color theory to select color combinations that create balance, contrast, and harmony. Color theory explains relationships between different colors and how they interact with each other.colors on the color wheel , how colors mix, and how different combinations can influence the way viewers interpret a design or piece of artwork.

Key Color theory Terms

  1. hue- The pure form of a color without tint,shade, or tone added
  2. color wheel- A circular arrangement of colors that shows the relationships between different hues
  3. Primary Colors- Colors that cannot be created by mixing other colors (red, blue, yellow).
  4. Secondary Colors- Colors created by mixing two primary colors (green, orange, purple).
  5. Tertiary Colors- Colors created by mixing a primary color with an adjacent secondary color (red-orange, yellow-orange, yellow-green, blue-green, blue-purple, red-purple).
  6. Tint- a lighter version of a color created by adding white.
  7. shade- A darker version of a color created by adding black.
  8. Tone- A color modified by adding gray.
  9. Saturation- THe intensity or purity of a color.
  10. Value (Brightness)- The lightness or darkness of a color.
  11. Warm Colors- Colors that feel warm, such as red, orange, and yellow.
  12. Cool Colors- Colors that feel cool, such as blue, green, and purple.
  13. Complementary Colors- Colors opposite each other on the color wheel that create strong contrast.
  14. Analogous Colors- Color next to each other on the color wheel that create harmony.
  15. Triadic Colors- three colors evenly spaced around the color wheel.
  16. Monochromatic Colors- variations of a single color using tints, tones, and shades.
  17. Split-Complementary- A color scheme using one color and two colors next to its complement.
  18. Color Harmony- A pleasing arrangement of colors that create balance.
  19. Contrast- The difference between colors that make elements stand out.
  20. Color Temperature- The perceived warmth or coolness of a color.
  21. Neutral Colors- Colors such as black, white, gray, and brown that are less intense.
  22. Opacity- The degree to which a color blocks light.
